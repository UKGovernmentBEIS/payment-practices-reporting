What if my financial year is not 12 months?

If your financial year is shorter or longer than twelve months by a few days or weeks, your second reporting period in a financial year may be slightly shorter or longer than six months as it will cover the remainder of the financial year. There are different rules if your financial year is 9 months or less or more than 15 months.

If you have shortened your accounting period to 9 months or less, you will only need to report once for that financial year. If you have extended your accounting reference period to more than 15 months, you will need to report 3 times, with the second report covering months 7 to 12 of your financial year, and the third report covering the remainder of the financial year.
